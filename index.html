About

My research aims to integrate human factors - behavioral, cognitive, and physiological - into human-centric artificial intelligence. Specifically, I lead human factors research at Seeing Machines to innovate real-time detection of driver states that are critical to road safety, such as distraction, drowsiness, and drunk driving. I use a mixture of statistical and machine learning approaches to analyzing and modeling driver states that are collected from simulation, test track, and large-scale naturalistic driving studies. The scientific findings have advanced commercial driver monitoring algorithms and been published in top research journals.
